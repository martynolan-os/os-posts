Frequently asked questions.

+What is Posts?
Posts is the purpose-built email replacement for artist-fan relationships, turning one-way email blasts into interactive fan experiences. Traditional email was built for broadcasting. Posts was built for participation. Every post lives on the web. You choose whether fans also get it in email, the feed, or both. Every interaction feeds into your fan graph. Substack knows subscribers. Openstage knows fans.
+Do Posts get sent to email automatically?
No. Every post publishes as a live web page automatically. From there, you choose: send to your email list, push to the feed, both, or neither. Each post is its own decision.
+What can you build?
Tour announcements, first-listen drops, album campaigns, merch exclusives, gated content, polls, pre-save unlocks, early access, community threads, fan acquisition campaigns.
+How is this different from Mailchimp or Beehiiv?
Posts is connected to your fan graph. Substack knows subscribers. Openstage knows fans. Plus Bandsintown, DSP embeds, presave blocks, and gating logic built for how artists release.
+How is this different from Broadcasts?
Broadcasts are email-only. Posts always publish to the web, and you choose whether to also send as email. New fans find it on the web. Every interaction feeds back into your fan graph.
+Are Posts replacing Broadcasts?
For email and web, yes. Broadcasts remain for SMS and lifecycle flows for now.
+Do fans need an account?
Depends on settings. Public, gated behind signup, gated to a specific audience, or paid subscribers. Per post and per block.
+What presave platforms are supported?
Spotify and Apple Music. YouTube and Amazon Music require linking out.
+How does data collection work?
Every view, click, poll response, comment, and signup flows into the fan graph automatically. No exports. No integrations.
+Can I gate individual blocks?
Yes. Lock a specific block behind signup while keeping the rest public. Signup happens in context.
+When can I start?
Posts is available now in open beta. New Openstage artists default to Posts from day one.
